Client Guide

Year-End Tax & Accounting Process

A clear, step-by-step guide to how QiAlly handles your year-end tax and accounting work—from engagement to filing.

Quick Navigation

00. Sign Engagement

Before any work begins, you'll sign an engagement letter that authorizes QiAlly Tax & Accounting to perform year-end and tax services on your behalf. This document outlines the scope of work, responsibilities, and fees.

What You Need to Do

  • Review the engagement letter carefully
  • Ask questions if anything is unclear
  • Sign and return the letter (digital signature accepted)

What QiAlly Handles

  • Prepare and send the engagement letter
  • Explain scope and timeline
  • Confirm receipt and store signed copy

01. Confirm Cutoff Date & Access

We confirm the accounting period to be included (typically through December 31) and ensure QiAlly has full access to your accounting system, bank records, and supporting documents.

What You Need to Do

  • Grant access to accounting software (QuickBooks, Xero, etc.)
  • Provide bank/credit card statements if needed
  • Confirm the year-end date (usually 12/31)

What QiAlly Handles

  • Verify access to all necessary systems
  • Document the cutoff date
  • Request any missing credentials or documents

02. Review Trial Balance

We review your trial balance for completeness, classification issues, and obvious anomalies before making any adjustments. This is our first diagnostic step.

What You Need to Do

  • Respond to questions about unusual transactions
  • Provide context for large or unexpected balances

What QiAlly Handles

  • Pull and analyze the trial balance
  • Identify classification errors or missing entries
  • Flag items for follow-up or adjustment

03. Post Adjusting Entries

We post required adjusting journal entries to correct classification, timing, or omissions. This ensures your books accurately reflect your financial position.

What You Need to Do

  • Review and approve proposed adjustments
  • Provide additional documentation if requested

What QiAlly Handles

  • Prepare adjusting journal entries
  • Post entries to the accounting system
  • Document rationale for each adjustment

04. Prepare Tax Return

We prepare your current-year tax return based on finalized books and any additional tax documents you've provided (W-2s, 1099s, K-1s, etc.).

What You Need to Do

  • Provide all tax documents (1099s, receipts, etc.)
  • Answer questions about deductions or credits
  • Confirm personal/business information is current

What QiAlly Handles

  • Prepare federal and state tax returns
  • Calculate tax liability or refund
  • Identify available deductions and credits

05. Tax Provision / Estimate

We calculate and record the tax provision or estimate tax liability, depending on your entity type. This ensures your financial statements reflect the expected tax obligation.

What You Need to Do

  • Review the estimated tax liability
  • Plan for payment if tax is owed

What QiAlly Handles

  • Calculate tax provision or estimate
  • Record entry in accounting system
  • Explain methodology and assumptions

06. Prepare Financial Statements

We prepare final financial statements (Balance Sheet, Income Statement, Cash Flow Statement) based on the adjusted trial balance. These statements give you a clear picture of your financial health.

What You Need to Do

  • Review the financial statements
  • Ask questions about any line items

What QiAlly Handles

  • Generate Balance Sheet and Income Statement
  • Prepare Cash Flow Statement (if applicable)
  • Format statements for clarity and presentation

07. Client Review & Approval

You review the final financial statements and tax return, and provide approval to proceed with filing. This is your opportunity to ask final questions and confirm accuracy.

What You Need to Do

  • Review financials and tax return thoroughly
  • Ask questions or request clarifications
  • Provide written approval to file

What QiAlly Handles

  • Present final package for review
  • Answer questions and make corrections if needed
  • Obtain formal approval before filing

08. File Tax Return

We file the approved tax return with the appropriate taxing authorities (IRS, state, local). This step is handled electronically for speed and accuracy.

What You Need to Do

  • Wait for confirmation of filing
  • Arrange payment if tax is owed

What QiAlly Handles

  • E-file the tax return
  • Submit to federal, state, and local authorities
  • Provide filing confirmation

09. Confirm Acceptance

We confirm acceptance or receipt by taxing authorities and resolve any rejections if needed. This ensures your return has been successfully processed.

What You Need to Do

  • Respond to any requests for additional information
  • Provide corrections if return is rejected

What QiAlly Handles

  • Monitor acceptance status
  • Resolve rejections or errors
  • Notify you once accepted

10. Lock Books

We lock the accounting period to prevent further changes after filing. This protects the integrity of your filed tax return and financial statements.

What You Need to Do

  • Understand that the period is now closed
  • Contact QiAlly if corrections are needed

What QiAlly Handles

  • Lock the accounting period in your system
  • Document the lock date
  • Maintain ability to unlock if truly necessary

11. Archive Year-End Package

We archive final financials, filed returns, and supporting documents for recordkeeping. This ensures you have a complete, organized record for future reference or audits.

What You Need to Do

  • Store your copy of the year-end package
  • Keep records for at least 7 years

What QiAlly Handles

  • Compile complete year-end package
  • Archive in secure, encrypted storage
  • Provide you with a copy for your records

Common Questions

How long does the entire process take?

Typically 2-6 weeks, depending on the complexity of your books and how quickly you can provide requested information.

What if I find an error after filing?

We can file an amended return if necessary. Contact us immediately if you discover an error.

Do I need to be involved in every phase?

We handle most of the technical work, but your input is critical during access setup, review, and approval phases.

What happens if my return is rejected?

We'll diagnose the issue, make corrections, and refile at no additional charge (assuming the rejection wasn't due to incorrect information you provided).
