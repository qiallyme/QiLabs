Module 1 – Basic Knowledge

Lesson 1: The Definition of a Service Dog

  • Legal Definition: A dog that is individually trained to do work or perform tasks for a person with a disability.
  • Gigi’s Role: Gigi is medical equipment. Her presence alone does not make her a service dog; her tasks (detecting panic, performing pressure therapy) do.

Lesson 2: Service Dog vs. ESA

  • ESA (Emotional Support Animal): requires no training; provides comfort by existing. Protected in Housing only.
  • Service Dog (Gigi): Requires intense training; mitigates a disability through specific actions. Protected in Housing AND Public Places (Stores, Restaurants, Planes).

Module 2 – Understanding Gigi

Lesson 1: The "Spoon Theory" for Dogs

  • Signs of Burnout: Slow to obey commands, excessive sniffing, refusing treats.
  • Rule: If Gigi is stressed, she cannot help with your stress. Training must stop if she shows "whale eye" or lip licking.

Lesson 2: Bonding & Engagement

  • The Umbilical Cord Method: For 3 days, keep Gigi on a leash attached to your belt while inside the house. She goes where you go.

Module 3 – Preparation & Vocabulary

Lesson 1: The Marker System

  • Tool: Clicker or "YES!"
  • Concept: Click/Say "YES" the moment she does right. Reward follows 1 second later.

Lesson 2: Essential Vocabulary

  • "Focus" / "Watch Me": Look at my eyes.
  • "Leave It": Ignore that smell/person/food.
  • "Break" / "Free": You are off duty now.

Module 4 – Foundation Skills

  1. Sit & Down: Hand signals and verbal cues.
  2. Stay: Duration before Distance.
  3. Heel: Walking by left leg without pulling.
  4. Tuck: Curling up small under a chair/table.

Module 5 – Public Access Obedience

Lesson 1: The "Invisible Dog" Standard

  • No Barking: Unless alerting to a medical episode.
  • No Sniffing: No sniffing groceries or people.
  • No Seeking Attention: No asking for pets while working.

Module 6 – PSD Core Tasks: Alert & Interruption

Lesson 1: The "Tell" (Identifying Triggers)

Identify what your body does before a panic attack (e.g., leg bouncing, face rubbing).

Lesson 2: Task 1 – The Interruption (Nudge)

  • Goal: Snap out of a pre-panic state.
  • How: Mimic "Tell," Click & Treat when she touches you with her nose.

Lesson 3: Task 2 – Blocking (Crowd Control)

  • Goal: Create space. Command: "Block" or "Cover."

Module 7 – PSD Core Tasks: Mitigation

Lesson 1: Task 3 – Deep Pressure Therapy (DPT)

  • Goal: Lower heart rate/regulate breathing. Command: "Pressure" or "Lap."

Lesson 2: Task 4 – Grounding (For Depressive States)

  • Goal: Physical stimulation to break dissociation. Command: "Visit" or "Touch."

Module 8 – Public Access Test (PAT) Checklist

  • [x] Unloads from vehicle calmly.
  • [x] Enters building without pulling.
  • [x] Ignores dropped food/noise.
  • [x] Holds a "Down-Stay" for 5 minutes.
  • [x] Recovers quickly from loud noise.

Module 9 – Assessment & Logging

  • Requirement: 120 training hours required (approx. 6 months).
  • Final Exam: Perform 1 Interruption task and 1 DPT task in a distracting environment.

Module 10 – Ongoing Reinforcement & Hardening

(Added Feb 01, 2026 - Post-Certification Phase)

  • Public Access Proofing: Weekly exposure to high-distraction environments (shopping malls, food courts) to maintain focus.
  • Extended DPT Duration: Increasing "Pressure" duration from 2 minutes to 10+ minutes to simulate real episode management.
  • Off-Leash Recall (Emergency): Practicing prompt recall in enclosed, safe areas with high distractions.
  • Handler-Down Drills: Simulating full incapacity to ensure Gigi remains close and alerts (pawing/nudging) rather than wandering.
  • Equipment Desensitization: Ensuring comfort with various harnesses, boots, and mobility gear.
