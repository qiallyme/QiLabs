# Extending Crisis Routing with the Resource & Opportunity Graph

The Resource & Opportunity Graph (ROG) extends the existing crisis engine by providing an additional, non-emergency layer of support for members whose primary issues are related to scarcity, lack of tools, or systemic financial waste.

## When to Involve the ROG

Navigators and Lina should consider ROG involvement when:

- The member’s crisis involves missing tools (no phone, no internet, no warm clothes) that block other help.  
- The member reports food insecurity, especially recurring.  
- The member suspects tax errors, wage theft, or unexplained loss of money.  
- The member expresses a desire to help others after receiving support.

## Routing Rules

- If severity ≥ 61 (high crisis or above), address immediate safety first. ROG offers may be queued but must not delay urgent intervention.  
- For 21 ≤ severity ≤ 60, ROG may be used in parallel with normal crisis navigation.  
- For low-severity, chronic issues (0–40), ROG may be the primary tool for stabilizing the situation.

## Examples

- A member fleeing DV has no coat or shoes for children → immediate safety response + ROG query for clothing offers.  
- A construction worker says they always “owe” at tax time despite low income → flag as `recoverable_funds` opportunity and route to professional partner.  
- A member just got stable housing but has no furniture → use ROG to search nearby offers + partners.

ROG does not replace the emergency engine — it complements it by turning community surplus and unlocked money into sustainable stability.
