# Chapter Two: Childhood

My childhood also was part of the reason why I started using drugs. My brother molesting me started when I was five. I remember him pulling down my pajamas, and rubbing on my bottom.

This happened at my grandma's house. There were happy times there as well. I loved my grandmother. we called her Mamma. My grandmother was the best cook. She took care of her house. It was always clean and unlike my mom who always had our homes tore up and daughters couldn't cook. I always wondered why my grandma never taught my mother to cook.

We grew up in Somerville Indiana but I was dragged back and forth from there to Indianapolis where I was born. My dad was from there and my mom was constantly going back to him. I liked staying at my grandma's because it was normal. Nothing was normal with my mom and dad. When I was five she took us back to Indianapolis to live with him. My dad.

I got to take some of my toys with me. I had a favorite doll crib that I took with me. My dad Claude had got me a record player and this is when I got my first Elvis records. Claude worked at RCA so every time he made a new song my dad would bring it home. The best gift my dad ever gave me was a black Chihuahua who was born with no tail. I named him Tiny. He bit me all the time but I loved him.

I believe I was six years old by now. This was the apartment where I remember my dad went to jail.

I don't know why I never called my mother mom. I called her Mary. All of us called her Mary. It may be because we didn't live with her first. My sister Molly and I were the only kids to live with my mom and dad. We called him Claude. He never asked me to call him dad. I wonder if that's why he didn't really talk to us.

My sister Anita and my brother Tony lived with our grandparents. We came down to stay with them off and on. Anita and I would play in her playhouse. We got along pretty good. I got along well with my brother as well. Even though we didn't see them often; I loved my brother despite what he did. We had good times as well.

My step grandfather worked for the railroad so he made good money. That's probably why our mom left us there a lot. I was scared of my grandfather because I felt he was mean to me. He would scare me by chasing me with the lawn mower and one day he chased me with the garden plow. Maybe he thought it was funny but after getting whipped by him with the belt I hated him at that time. I guess that's why I wished him dead when I was 11 years old. He died about two weeks later. My grandma found him in the outhouse dead of a heart attack. This was kind of ironic because he never installed a toilet in the house. we had to use the outhouse during the day and pots at the time which my grandmother cleaned every morning.

It was fun growing up in Somerville. we spent a lot of time at the creek. My brother and I would pick blackberries and bring them home. My grandma would make us blackberry pie. My grandpa would always be working in the garden when he was home. we could pick strawberries and eat them and we had a grapevine. we always had all the garden vegetables we could eat. It wasn't bad living there.

Grandma would whip us from time to time. She would make us go get a switch when we were bad. I wanted to call the welfare department to complain but the welts would disappear in five to ten minutes. That made me mad.

But I later felt sorry for my grandma. This is the first time I found out my mom was crazy. She had been screaming and arguing with my grandma all that day. My mom would not stop. She was relentless and I wondered what on earth my grandma could have done. My grandma was just sitting in a kitchen chair and was crying her eyes out. So finally, my mom stopped and went into the bedroom. She was in there for a while when she came out she had taken scissors and cut off all her hair. The next thing I know my grandma called the sheriff's and they came and took my mom away to the state hospital. My mom would later claim they gave her shock treatments. I thought she might have gone crazy after the Greyhound bus wreck.

That day I remember watching out the door for her to come back from Indianapolis. This was right before we moved there. She was on a bus and it was foggy and icy that morning. My mom had just finished holding a newborn baby when the bus driver all of a sudden saw another car right in front of his bus and swerved so he wouldn't hit it. The bus rolled over three times and that baby was killed. She never came home that day, but when she did come home she had a neck brace on. Because I was five I thought her head had been cut off and sewed back on. I had a nightmare that my mom was on her hands and knees scrubbing my grandma's kitchen floor and my grandma accidentally bumped her and her head came off and rolled across the kitchen floor. Then I would wake up.

After all that I was the first one to move to Indy to live with my mom and dad. The day he went to jail my mom had cooked us dinner. I remember the whole kernel corn and mashed potatoes. My dad told her to never put pepper in his food because it reminded him of dirt. He had a weak stomach. She had put it in the corn and mashed potatoes. He got mad and he was probably drunk because the next thing I remember is corn and mashed potatoes being thrown all over the kitchen walls. Eventually my mom called the police. He had a knife and cut one of the police officers' hands. I remember the house being trashed and blood everywhere. When they took him away I thought he was gone for good.

The only other thing I can remember is my dad did get out of jail. I don't remember how long he was gone. I do have some good memories of the school that was across the street. when it was raining or snowing instead of recess outside we would go to the auditorium and watch a movie. I remember one day a black kid ran up to me and kissed me on the cheek. I didn't know why he did that but I thought it was nice.

My grandpa was very prejudiced against black people. I witnessed this when I was still living at my grandpa's house. we had a station wagon and we had to go into town to shop at a major store. So, we went into the local store. As we pulled into the parking lot and got out of the car there was a group of black people going into the store, and my grandpa started calling them the N word. I asked my brother why he was doing that because I could tell he didn't even know them. This was the first time I knew what prejudice was but I did know that I didn't like it. That is not the only bad thing that happened that day.

When we went into the store I saw a jar of hot tamales and I was begging my grandpa to buy it for me. He eventually said okay and put it in the cart. when we had supper that night my grandma gave me my hot tamales. I did not like them at all, but because I had such a fit my grandpa made me sit there and eat them. I have not eaten them since. But I hated him for that too.

Back in Indy my next memory I have is an upstairs apartment where there was a bathroom in the hall which was shared between tenants. My dad would hide his vodka bottle in the clothes hamper there. I know he kept one under the seat in the car too. My mom and dad fought a lot in that apartment. I remember one time they were fighting and he ended up on top of her in the bed. I don't remember what he was doing but to make him stop I went and got a broom and hit him as hard as I could on the ankle with the pole part of the broom. He stopped and came after me and picked me up and threw me on the couch.

Me and my mom left that night and took a taxi to a friend's house that she knew. I know she always called the police but they never did anything about him.

My next memory in this apartment is when dad was drinking vodka and orange juice. At first, he was a funny drunk, but as my mom started picking at him he would turn mean. Next thing I know she started throwing dishes and silverware at him. well eventually a plate or cup knocked him out. I thought he was dead and I was glad.

He didn't die that night he eventually woke up. I remember this is where my mom found out she was pregnant with my youngest sister. My dad was so proud that he got her pregnant because he was in his late fifties. we moved again and I remember this is the house where I was starting to notice my mom was still crazy. My sister Molly came to live with us. My dad bought us bicycles to ride. Molly and I used to ride up and down this street. I was glad to finally have a sister living with us.

I know this place was pretty run down. There was still trash and junk in the backyard and this recliner was back there. There were baby mice in it. My mom started saving these mice in jars. She was counting them. She was also saving milk cartons for some reason. I thought maybe because they had pictures of people missing on them. My mom's mental illness was starting to get worse.

My youngest sister was born around this time. My dad wasn't living with us but he came to visit my sisters and myself. Next, we moved to the south side in Indianapolis. we lived there the longest. I remember the school wasn't too far and I was walking to school.

My oldest sister was living with us too. She is my half-sister but I always looked at her as my real sister. I know she had a friend across the street and so did I. I had to be about seven years old because this was the year I saw Elvis at the coliseum.

My friend Cecelia and I got along pretty well and Mary let me spend the night with her. I got my first glimpse of what a normal household was like.

I was getting tired of being poor and my mom had us on welfare and food stamps. She would always want to take us window shopping which I hated because we could never buy anything. we got Christmas presents from the welfare I guess and a lot of times these toys were used.

One time while living here my dad came home. It might have been because he was sick. He had an abscess on his pancreas from the alcohol. After the surgery he stayed in bed a lot. I guess then he must have gone back to work. That year he bought a mustang or at least already had it I don't know. I believe this is the time they had a crash and the radio knob was embedded in my mom's knee. After that she was terrified of riding in any vehicle.

I still remember he started drinking and I think my mom was accusing him of being in a strip bar. I remember her talking about a G-String and a place called Red Garter. When the black kids came I liked them. I had seen black people but I didn't know anyone black except for people I would see in Indianapolis.

So, I was excited to get to know the kids that came to our school. I liked them. It seemed that they were fun and just like me some of them were poor. we had the poor people lunch at our school. Free lunch program I guess was the appropriate term. we could get breakfast too.
