DESCRIPTION:
"This is not a story about redemption. It’s a story about survival, power, and the cost of escape."

From the metallic tang of poverty in rural Indiana to the neon-soaked underworld of Phoenix, "Paid In Full" is a visceral memoir of a woman who refused to be a victim. Raised in a home where safety was a moving target, she escaped into a marriage with a "Monster" who kept her in chains—both real and psychological. 

Escaping into the world of stripping and high-stakes escorting, she traded one form of control for another, mastering the "economics of desire" while navigating a landscape of pimps, cocaine, and violence. But when a routine drive turns into a life-or-death struggle against a knife-wielding predator, she is forced to shatter the last of her illusions.

This is a raw, unflinching look at the "Invisible Child," the "invisible economy," and the price of true autonomy.
